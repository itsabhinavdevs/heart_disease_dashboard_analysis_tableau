Key Takeaway

Heart disease is a preventable condition for a significant portion of the population. The data shows that modifiable risk factors — smoking, physical inactivity, obesity, and excessive alcohol — account for a substantial share of heart disease cases. Targeted interventions addressing these factors can meaningfully reduce cardiovascular disease burden.
